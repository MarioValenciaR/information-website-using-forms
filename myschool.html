University of Ottawa - Campus Ottawa

Our history

The University of Ottawa (UOttawa), located in the heart of Ottawa, Ontario, the capital of Canada, was established in 1848, making it one of the oldest universities in the country. Initially founded as a Catholic institution, it has since evolved into a secular, publicly funded research university. UOttawa is recognized as the largest bilingual (English-French) university in the world, offering students the option to study in English, French, or a combination of both languages.

Programs Offered

  • Faculty of Arts: Programs in humanities, social sciences, and fine arts.
  • Telfer School of Management: Offers business programs including BCom, MBA, and executive education.
  • Faculty of Education: Specializes in teacher education, research, and educational leadership.
  • Faculty of Engineering: Offers programs in civil, mechanical, electrical, computer, and software engineering, among others.
  • Faculty of Law: Divided into common law and civil law sections, offering bilingual legal education.
  • Faculty of Medicine: One of the top medical schools in Canada, offering MD programs, research, and residency training.
  • Faculty of Science: Offers courses in biology, chemistry, mathematics, physics, and more.
  • Faculty of Social Sciences: Focuses on programs like psychology, sociology, economics, and political science.
  • Faculty of Health Sciences: Offers programs in nursing, human kinetics, and rehabilitation.

Facilities

Libraries

Libraries: UOttawa is home to an extensive network of libraries, including specialized libraries for science, law, and health, all providing access to millions of books, journals, and digital resources.

Research

Laboratories and Research Facilities: UOttawa is a leader in research, and its labs are equipped for cutting-edge research in various fields including engineering, science, health, and social sciences.

Sports and Recreation

UOttawa offers a range of athletic facilities, including the Minto Sports Complex, swimming pools, fitness centers, and fields for soccer, rugby, and track. The Gee-Gees sports teams participate in various varsity-level sports.
